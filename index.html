About

My career path hasn't been the most straightforward, but my varied career offers me a unique perspective and a wealth of transferable skills. After spending seven years as a full stack engineer, I took a career break to pursue some personal goals and spend time with my young family. After some time in the property and brewing industries it's time for me to resume my technical career and apply my skills and experience to a new challenge.

After seven years as a developer at the same company it was time for a new challenge. Before taking another software job I took the opportunity to pursue my dream of property development. I quickly learnt all the required skills to fully renovate properties myself and completed four successful renovation projects, only calling in professionals for gas and electrical works. By the time I'd finished the fourth property project I had two young children, so I took some time off work to spend with them.

During this time I also achieved another personal goal by starting my own microbrewery. I'm incredibly proud of what I accomplished, but unfortunately this experience did sidetrack my career, and I ended up staying in the brewing industry for longer than I anticipated when a friend offered me a job at Ilkley Brewery. Whilst I enjoyed brewing, I missed the intellectual stimulation, and I am now eager to return to my technical career, and to apply my skills and experience to new challenges.

Professional Experience

Full stack engineer

2008 - 2015

TPP, Leeds

I spent seven years as a full stack engineer on one of the largest real-time databases in the world. I was responsible for developing and maintaining software which handled millions of patient medical records. During this time, I developed expertise in a wide range of technical areas:

  • Java development as part of an agile team
  • Implementing technical requirements through the full software development lifecycle
  • Database design and maintenance (MS SQL Server), including SQL stored procedures
  • Server monitoring and application optimisation using various tools (memory snapshots, SQL traces, app profiles, etc)
  • GUI design and implementation (MVVM)

As a senior engineer, I led the technical data migration team and was responsible for:

  • Ensuring the team’s tasks from the internal ticketing system were resolved in good time
  • Creating custom ETL pipelines to accurately migrate patient medical records
  • Creating and maintaining data architecture to support our implementation of the new GP2GP requirements, as well as mass migration of an entire practice’s medical records
  • Integration with other software using API’s and via XML messaging using NHS SPINE

I also developed soft skills such as:

  • Leadership – I led and organised a team of up to 18 engineers, including training of new starters, performance reviews and mentorship.
  • Communication and collaboration – working with our direct competitors to ensure the smooth migration of data, and attending meetings with external stakeholders to secure new business.
  • Planning and organisation – I contributed to the company’s operational plans and ensured that my team had the required resources to complete our workload in the given time frame.
